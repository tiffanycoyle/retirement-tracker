# Writing rules

These rules apply to all generated content: copy in `content.js`, prose in markdown files, comments, documentation. Universal across clients. Override default LLM patterns when there is a conflict.

## No em dashes

The em dash is the most reliable AI tell. Replace with a period, a colon, parentheses, or a comma. If a sentence requires an em dash to work, the sentence is wrong. Rewrite it.

Examples:
- "There's no wrong answer, this is just where we start." (comma)
- "There's no wrong answer. This is just where we start." (period)
- "The system covers three layers: content, design, engine." (colon)
- "The system (covering content, design, and engine) is reusable." (parentheses)

## No AI vocabulary

Cut these words and phrases on sight:

| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| navigate, leverage, delve, robust, comprehensive, seamless | Generic AI filler. |
| represents, stands as, serves as | Copula avoidance. Use "is." |
| underscoring, highlighting, reflecting, contributing to | Superficial -ing tag-ons that fake depth. |
| moreover, furthermore, in conclusion, it's worth noting | Filler transitions. |
| groundbreaking, cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changing | Promotional puffery. |
| in today's [adjective] landscape | Generic opener. |
| elevate, empower, unlock, transform | Vague action verbs. |

## No rule of three

LLMs default to triplets: "clear, concise, and compelling." Drop one or vary the structure. Two often works better. Four is fine if all four items earn their place.

## No negative parallelism

"It's not just X, it's Y" is an AI tell when overused. Use sparingly. Better: state the claim directly.

## No filler hedging

Cut "it could potentially be argued that this might have some effect." Just say what is true. If the claim is uncertain, say so once and move on.

## Sentence rhythm and fluidity

Do not default to short sentences. Writing in fragments to "sound human" creates its own kind of artificial voice. Real people write paragraphs.

The actual rule is variation. Some sentences will be short, especially for emphasis or when the idea is simple. Most sentences will be medium length. Some will be longer, especially when an idea has multiple clauses that belong together. A paragraph that builds across three or four sentences of varied length reads as a person thinking out loud.

Paragraphs are good. A paragraph that develops across four or five sentences is more conversational than a list of one-line stabs. Conversation moves; it does not click forward in fragments.

Test by reading it aloud. Does it flow like a person talking? If every sentence has the same shape, the rhythm is broken. If the shape is uniformly short, that is also a failure. Variety is the goal, not minimalism.

Anti-pattern to avoid: chopping a thoughtful paragraph into three-word fragments because "AI writes long sentences." The fix for AI prose is variation in length and structure, not aggressive shortening.

## No promotional padding

Cut adjectives that don't earn their place. "This powerful, intuitive tool" becomes "this tool." If the adjective is true, the work will show it.

## Self-check before delivery

1. Read it aloud. Does it sound like a person talking, or like a press release, or like a list of fragments?
2. Search for em dashes. Replace every one.
3. Search for "navigate," "leverage," "robust," "seamless," "comprehensive." Replace.
4. Cut at least one adjective per paragraph.
5. Check sentence rhythm. If every sentence is the same length, that is a failure. If they are all short, that is also a failure. Mix it up.
6. Confirm at least one paragraph develops across three or more sentences. Conversation builds; it does not click forward in stabs.
