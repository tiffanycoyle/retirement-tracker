# Marcus voice samples

These are sentences Marcus actually wrote. Match this voice. Match the rhythm, the specificity, the confidence, the way he names a problem and then names what to do about it. No description, no commentary, no analysis. Just his voice.

Read these before writing any user-facing copy. Every line of copy in `content.js` should sound like it could have been pulled from this file.

---

I spend the first 20 minutes of every intro call walking through the same math on a whiteboard.

Income, expenses, savings rate, retirement target, gap.

It's the same conversation 60 times a year.

By the time we get to the actual planning, I've burned a third of the meeting on arithmetic.

They want to know if they're on track for retirement, if they're saving enough, if they should be worried.

It's the question underneath every other question they ask me.

It doesn't need to be perfect financial modeling.

It needs to be honest, clear, and useful enough that someone walks away with a real answer to "are we okay?"

This is the part most calculators get wrong. They show someone they're $400K behind and leave them there.

People close the tab and don't come back.

When someone's behind, the tool should tell them they're behind, but it should also show them what closing the gap actually looks like.

How much more per month would they need to save? What if they retired two years later?

Give them a path, not a verdict.

It can't feel like a bank calculator. Those things are sterile. People don't trust them and they don't engage with them.

I want it to feel like talking to me. Honest when the news is good. Direct when it isn't.

A real human walking someone through their numbers.

If a 42 year old with two kids and a mortgage opens this thing, they should feel like someone who actually understands their life is on the other side of it.

Don't overbuild this. I said it already but I'll say it again.
